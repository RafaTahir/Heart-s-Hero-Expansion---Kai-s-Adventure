# Kai's Adventure visual system

## Direction

Kai's Adventure feels like an illuminated pop-up storybook: tactile terrain, carved controls, hand-shaped silhouettes, and light that changes the world. Screens are places rather than dashboards. The mountain is the visual anchor; Kai and Pip are the emotional anchor.

## Color

- Ink night `#142333`: foundational depth and readable contrast.
- Mist blue `#7898a4`: the faded world's quiet atmosphere.
- Parchment `#fff1c7`: warm reading surfaces.
- Sunrise `#ffc65c`: guidance, focus, and restored light.
- Courage coral `#ef765d`: brave action and mountain accents.
- Mineral cyan `#72d7d4`: crystal illumination.
- Forest teal `#247f73`: kindness and living paths.
- Star blue `#344f91`: perseverance and night depth.

Color never carries state alone; every region includes a word and shape cue.

## Type

Fraunces is the locally bundled story display face. Nunito is the locally bundled reading and control face. Headings are short, warm, and irregular; body copy stays at 16px or larger and child actions at 18px or larger.

## Shape and composition

The visual grammar uses rounded trail lines, carved plaques, irregular hill silhouettes, compass circles, leaf arcs, and star facets. Layouts are full-bleed scenes with one dominant action and a lower reading shelf. No floating dashboard card grids, glass panels, stock imagery, or generic gradients.

## Motion

Motion explains cause and effect: Pip points toward a path, light follows, the landscape responds. Small interactions use explicit transform/opacity transitions. Reduced motion removes pulsing, drift, parallax, and staged restoration while preserving the final illustrated state and announcements.

## Voice

Child-facing copy is calm, brave, and concrete. Captions use no more than two short sentences. Choices are expressive and never scored. Grown-up copy is direct and makes no security or account claim.
