# Kai's Adventure — Demo Script

**Target length:** 100–130 seconds

## Before recording

1. Open the production URL in a clean browser profile at 1280×800.
2. Visit `/reset`, confirm the reset, then return to `/`.
3. Hide extensions, bookmarks, personal data, and developer tooling.
4. Enable captions. Use no music unless its rights are documented.

## Recording beats

**0:00–0:12 — The problem**  
“Values can feel abstract to a child. Kai's Adventure turns one small real-world act into a visible change they can own.” Show Kai and Pip facing the faded world.

**0:12–0:25 — One-minute setup**  
Choose Ages 8–9, Feeling worried, and Quick Quest. “A grown-up picks what fits today. Progress stays on this device; there is no account and no timer.”

**0:25–0:48 — Story choice**  
Show the two sleeping regions, enter the Mountain of Echoes, and choose “Call into the cave,” then “Take a small step.” Say that choices are expressive paths, not right/wrong tests.

**0:48–1:05 — Real-world bridge**  
Accept “Do one small thing that feels difficult.” Return to the map to show the saved mission, open it again, and select “I did it.”

**1:05–1:28 — Signature payoff**  
Let the Mountain transformation play: Pip's trail, quiet echoes, sunrise, crystals, compass, reward. Mention the always-visible skip and immediate reduced-motion version.

**1:28–1:43 — Progress**  
Collect the Courage Compass. Show the restored map, both newly open paths, the collection, and grown-up reflection prompt.

**1:43–1:58 — Technical truth**  
“This is a strict static React/TypeScript app with validated local quest packs, a pure reducer, versioned recovery, original repository-owned SVGs, and injected persistence/content/lexicon boundaries. GPT-5.6 and Codex helped build it; no model, backend, key, or runtime generation ships in the product.”

**Close**  
Show the public URL and `/reset`. “One brave act. A world visibly restored.”
